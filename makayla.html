My Leadership Philosophy

Leadership is a dynamic journey, one that requires the ability to alternate between being a follower and a leader. To lead effectively, it is essential to first experience the role of a follower, as this provides a foundational understanding of teamwork, empathy, and the diverse perspectives fostered by different people and communities. By following, one learns the intricacies of group dynamics, the importance of listening, and the value of supporting others, all of which are critical skills for any effective leader.

My leadership philosophy is deeply rooted in the principle of inclusive and respectful collaboration. I am dedicated to hearing all sides of an issue and valuing every team member's input. This approach promotes serenity and ensures that decisions are well-rounded and considerate of all perspectives. Open dialogue and mutual respect are not just cornerstones but the very foundation of my leadership style, as constructive communication, is key to resolving conflicts and achieving common goals. In the face of challenges, I focus on what is possible, even when situations appear impossible. This involves maintaining a positive outlook and a solution-oriented mindset, which are crucial for overcoming obstacles and driving progress. By concentrating on opportunities and potential solutions, leaders can inspire their teams to persevere and innovate, turning adversity into success.

Moreover, effective leadership requires adaptability and a willingness to learn continuously. The ability to evolve and grow in response to new experiences and feedback is vital for staying relevant and effective. As a leader, I strive to embody these qualities, encouraging continuous improvement and fostering an environment where everyone feels empowered to contribute their best. In essence, my approach to leadership is multifaceted, encompassing learning, empathy, collaboration, resilience, and adaptability. By integrating these elements, I aim to inspire and guide others toward achieving shared goals and creating a positive impact. Through this journey, I seek to lead and cultivate an environment where leadership is a shared and collective endeavour.

My Inspirational Leaders

- Jody Wilson Raybould

Jody Wilson-Raybould, born March 23, 1971, and known by her initials JWR and her Kwak'wala name Puglaas, is a prominent Canadian lawyer, author, and former politician. She served as the Member of Parliament (MP) for the British Columbia (BC) riding of Vancouver Granville from 2015 to 2021. Initially elected as a member of the Liberal Party, she held the significant roles of Justice Minister and Attorney General from 2015 to 2019. In 2019, she briefly served as Veterans Minister and Associate National Defence Minister.

Wilson-Raybould's tenure in the Liberal Party ended abruptly during the SNC-Lavalin affair when she resigned in response to statements from Prime Minister Justin Trudeau. Following her resignation, she was expelled from the Liberal caucus but continued to serve in Parliament as an Independent. She was reelected as an Independent MP in 2019, demonstrating her resilience and popularity. However, she chose not to run for re-election in 2021.Before her federal political career, Jody Wilson-Raybould made significant contributions at the provincial level as a BC Crown prosecutor. She also played an influential role as a treaty commissioner and served as the regional chief of the BC Assembly of First Nations. Throughout her career, Wilson-Raybould has been a staunch advocate for Indigenous rights and justice reform, leaving a lasting impact on Canadian politics and law.

Quote

“I come from a long line of matriarchs, and that’s what gave me strength to do what I needed to do. The strength that we have as Indigenous Peoples, and particularly as Indigenous women, is that we show the way. We guide the path. We guide chiefs. That’s my role in the Big House. And that’s what’s carried me through this last tumultuous year.”


Jody Wilson-Raybould's story is one of profound depth and resilience, rooted in a heritage of strong matriarchs and a rich indigenous history. Growing up within a family deeply connected to their ancestral traditions, she absorbed invaluable lessons from the wisdom of her foremothers, who supported and guided her throughout her life. Transitioning from the governance structures of her Indigenous community to the intricate workings of federal politics was a journey filled with challenges and opportunities. With each step, she carried with her the teachings of her ancestors, navigating the complexities of this transition with grace and determination. Wilson-Raybould's journey is one of personal growth and profound societal significance. Her transition symbolizes the intersection of Indigenous heritage with the modern world, showcasing the resilience of Indigenous peoples in adapting to change while preserving their cultural identity.

In her book and her public service, Wilson-Raybould exemplifies the strength of character and integrity required to navigate the corridors of power in Parliament. Her advocacy for indigenous rights and her commitment to justice have left an indelible mark on Canadian politics, inspiring countless individuals who admire her dedication and courage. Through her experiences and achievements, Jody Wilson-Raybould stands as a beacon of hope and empowerment, demonstrating the transformative potential of embracing one's heritage while forging new paths in the pursuit of a better future for all. Ultimately, I feel I relate to her in some ways.




- David Goggins



David Goggins is a remarkable individual whose life story is as inspiring as it is captivating. Born on February 17, 1975, he has carved a path of resilience and determination across various domains, from serving in the military to conquering ultra-endurance challenges. Raised in Williamsville, New York, Goggins experienced a tumultuous childhood marked by severe abuse inflicted by his father. Despite these early hardships, he found solace in hard work, assisting his family at his father's skate rink from a young age. His escape from this abusive environment alongside his mother showcases his inner strength and resolve, a theme he explores candidly in his memoir, "Can't Hurt Me."

Goggins's journey into the military was not without its hurdles. Initially aiming to join the United States Air Force Pararescue, he encountered setbacks, including a diagnosis of sickle cell trait during training. Undeterred, he redirected his focus to the United States Air Force Tactical Air Control Party (TACP), where he served from 1994 to 1999 before transitioning to the United States Navy SEALs. His path to becoming a Navy SEAL was paved with challenges, including multiple attempts to pass the rigorous BUD/S training. However, his relentless determination paid off when he graduated from BUD/S class 235 in 2001. Subsequently, he completed SEAL Qualification Training and earned the esteemed SEAL Trident, marking the beginning of a distinguished military career.Over two decades of service, Goggins deployed to Iraq and Afghanistan, demonstrating exemplary courage and resilience in the face of adversity. His commitment to excellence was further underscored by his graduation from Army Ranger School in 2004, where he earned the prestigious "Top Enlisted Man" award.

Beyond his military achievements, Goggins has become renowned for his feats of endurance, including ultra-marathons, ultra-distance cycling, and triathlons. His relentless pursuit of excellence and his unwavering dedication to pushing the limits of human potential have earned him widespread admiration and respect. Through his book, public speaking engagements, and personal achievements, David Goggins continues to inspire countless individuals worldwide to embrace discomfort, challenge their perceived limitations, and strive for greatness in every aspect of life.
